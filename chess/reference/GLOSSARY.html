Chess Glossary

The shared vocabulary for every lesson in this workspace. Terms here are used with these exact meanings throughout. Revisit it often — recognising the name of a pattern is the first step to spotting it on the board.

Material — what the pieces are worth

A rough trading scale. Use it to answer "is this capture good for me?"

  • ♟ Pawn = 1
  • ♞ Knight = 3
  • ♝ Bishop = 3
  • ♜ Rook = 5
  • ♛ Queen = 9
  • ♚ King = ∞ (game over)

Blunders & safety

Blunder
A serious mistake that loses material or the game — most often, leaving a piece where it can be captured for free. The thing we are training to eliminate first.
Hanging piece
A piece that is attacked and not defended — it can be taken for free. Your job every move: make sure none of yours is hanging.
En prise
French for "in take" — a piece left undefended and capturable. Same idea as hanging.
CCT — Checks, Captures, Threats
The scan you run on your opponent's options (and your own): what Checks, Captures, and Threats exist in the position right now. The backbone of the Blunder-Check.

Tactics (winning material by force)

Fork
One piece attacks two (or more) targets at once. Knights fork famously.
Pin
A piece can't move because a more valuable piece sits behind it on the same line.
Skewer
Like a pin reversed: the valuable piece is in front and must move, exposing the one behind.
Discovered attack
Moving one piece unveils an attack from a piece behind it. A discovered check is the nastiest version.

Opening & general terms

Development
Getting your knights and bishops off the back rank into active squares. Castle early.
Tempo
A unit of time = one move. You "gain a tempo" when you make a useful move that also forces your opponent to react.
Castling
King + rook move that tucks the king to safety. Do it early unless there's a reason not to.
En passant
Special pawn capture: if an enemy pawn jumps two squares past your pawn, you may capture it as if it moved one — but only immediately.

Endgame terms (for later)

Opposition
Kings face each other with one square between them; the player not to move "has the opposition" and controls key squares. Vital in king-and-pawn endings.
Promotion
A pawn reaching the far rank becomes a queen (usually). The point of most endgames.
