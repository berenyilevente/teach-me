# Mission: Climb ~300 rating points in chess (blitz & rapid)

## Why
Levente plays online chess (≈750 blitz / ≈950 rapid) and wants to genuinely get
stronger — to stop losing games to avoidable mistakes and start winning more.
The concrete target is a sustained rating gain of roughly +300 in both formats.

## Success looks like
- Goes whole games without hanging a piece or missing a free capture — runs a
  blunder-check on every move almost automatically.
- Can stay focused for a full rapid game instead of drifting and playing on autopilot.
- Plays the **London System** confidently as White from memory through the opening.
- Has **one solid defence as Black** against 1.e4 (and a plan vs 1.d4) and knows its ideas.
- Spots common middlegame tactics (forks, pins, skewers, discovered attacks) in their own games.
- Knows the basic endgames every club player must know (K+Q vs K, K+R vs K, king & pawn, opposition).
- Has filled the obvious knowledge gaps a sub-1000 player typically has.

## Constraints
- Self-taught online; learning across multiple sessions. Time per session is short —
  lessons must be quick and immediately usable in the next game.
- Primary practice arena: online blitz (3–5 min) and rapid (10–30 min).

## Out of scope (for now)
- Deep opening theory / long memorised lines. We want setups and ideas, not 20-move trees.
- Advanced positional concepts beyond what's needed to stop blundering and win material.
- Becoming a tournament/OTB competitor (can revisit if the goal changes).
