# Chess Improvement Resources

## Knowledge

### Foundations & blunder-prevention (priority)
- [YouTube: "Habits" / "Building Habits" — Aman Hambleton (ChessBrah)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLkrL9SXJDsCQT5lExgB9bnPp7zEvegz0R)
  GM plays up from beginner ratings using strict simple rules (don't hang pieces, develop,
  trade when ahead). The single best resource for the "stop blundering" mission. Use for:
  watching a strong player's move-by-move safety thinking at your exact level.
- [YouTube: Daniel Naroditsky "Speedrun" series](https://www.youtube.com/@DanielNaroditskyGM)
  GM narrates his full thought process while playing/teaching low-rated games. Use for:
  hearing *how* to think during a game; calm, instructive.

### Tactics training
- [Lichess Puzzles (free)](https://lichess.org/training)
  Unlimited free puzzles, adapts to your level, themed puzzles (forks, pins, etc.).
  Use for: daily tactics reps and motif-specific drilling.
- [Chess.com Puzzles & Puzzle Rush](https://www.chess.com/puzzles)
  Use for: daily tactics + timed Puzzle Rush. Either platform is fine — pick one.
- [Article: "Improve your Tactics with Puzzles" — Chess.com](https://www.chess.com/article/view/improve-your-tactics-with-puzzles)
  Use for: how to actually train tactics (not just rack up a rating).

### Openings
- [Article: "Best Chess Openings for Black for Beginners" — Chess.com](https://www.chess.com/article/view/black-chess-openings-for-beginners)
  Use for: comparing Black defences (Caro-Kann recommended by Nakamura as best beginner opening).
- [Caro-Kann complete guide — TheChessWorld](https://thechessworld.com/articles/openings/caro-kann-defense-complete-opening-guide/)
  Use for: ideas, main lines, and plans of the Caro-Kann.
- [London System guide — ChessBase 30-minute training](https://en.chessbase.com/post/learn-to-play-the-london-system-opening)
  Use for: a fast, trustworthy intro to the London setup and ideas.
- [London System full guide — Chess-Teacher](https://chess-teacher.com/london-system-opening-guide/)
  Use for: deeper London lines once the basic setup is solid.

### Endgames (for later sessions)
- Book: *Silman's Complete Endgame Course* — Jeremy Silman.
  The standard club-player endgame book, organised by rating so you only learn what you
  need now. Use for: basic checkmates, king & pawn, opposition. (Find legally / library.)

## Wisdom (Communities)
- [r/chess](https://reddit.com/r/chess) and [r/chessbeginners](https://reddit.com/r/chessbeginners)
  r/chessbeginners is the friendlier place to post your games for feedback. Use for:
  posting an annotated loss and asking "where did I go wrong?"
- [Lichess Study + forums](https://lichess.org/forum)
  Use for: sharing analysis, asking opening/endgame questions.
- (Optional) A local chess club — strongest source of real-game wisdom if budget/time allow.

## Gaps
- Need a concrete Black plan vs 1.d4 / non-1.e4 openings (Caro-Kann only answers 1.e4).
- Want one trusted, specific Caro-Kann line video/course for the main lines (Advance, Exchange,
  Two Knights, Fantasy) pitched at ~1000 level — find before the Caro-Kann lesson.
