# Baseline established: shaky, region-by-region, news-driven

Berenyi self-rates as **shaky**: can confidently name ~a dozen countries, continents
are fuzzy, and capitals are mostly blank. This sets the floor — we cannot assume any
region knowledge, so teaching starts from the global skeleton (continents + oceans)
before any region work.

His motivation is **following the news, social confidence, and self-respect** (not
travel, not academia). This means we prioritise news-relevant countries and the
"roughly where is it / who are its neighbours" layer over exhaustive coverage.

He chose **region by region** as the progression style. Honoured, with the caveat that
a one-lesson global skeleton comes first because regions need a frame to attach to.

Implication: keep lessons concrete and headline-grounded; defer obscure states; build
the mental map outward from a sound continental frame. See [[MISSION.md]].
