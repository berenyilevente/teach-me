# Mission: Practical World Geography

## Why
Berenyi wants to follow the news without reaching for a map, hold his own in
conversation when a country or region comes up, and close a knowledge gap he
feels an educated adult should not have. The goal is a durable mental map of the
world — not academic depth.

## Success looks like
- Can place all 7 continents and 5 oceans from memory, instantly.
- Hears a country in the news and knows roughly where it is and who its neighbours are.
- Can name and locate the major countries of each region, plus their capitals.
- Recognises the flags of the ~30 countries that dominate headlines.
- Doesn't feel caught out when a place is mentioned in conversation.

## Constraints
- Wants practical, real-world knowledge — NOT academic geography (no geomorphology, climatology, etc.).
- Starting baseline is shaky: can confidently name ~a dozen countries; continents are fuzzy; capitals mostly blank.
- Prefers to learn region by region (after an initial global skeleton, which the shaky baseline requires).
- Lessons should be short and completable quickly.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Physical/academic geography (plate tectonics, biomes, river systems as a science).
- Obscure microstates and trivia that never appear in news or conversation.
- Memorising every one of the world's ~195 countries — we prioritise the news-relevant ones first.
