The World Skeleton

7 continents and 5 oceans — the frame every other place hangs on.

The 7 continents, largest to smallest

#ContinentWhere it sitsNews anchor
1AsiaEast half of the big landmass; north of the Indian OceanChina, India, Middle East, most of Russia
2AfricaSouth of Europe, across the Mediterranean; straddles the equatorEgypt, Nigeria, Sahel, South Africa
3North AmericaTop-left of a world map; west of the AtlanticUSA, Canada, Mexico
4South AmericaBelow North America; mostly in the southern hemisphereBrazil, Argentina, Venezuela
5AntarcticaThe bottom of the map; the South PoleNo countries — research bases only
6EuropeNorth-west peninsula of the big landmass; "the West" + Russia's edgeEU, Ukraine, UK
7Australia / OceaniaBottom-right; south-east of Asia, in the PacificAustralia, New Zealand, Pacific islands

Frame trick: Asia + Europe + Africa are one connected "Old World" landmass. The Americas are a second strip on the left. Australia + Antarctica float at the bottom. Russia spans Europe and Asia; Egypt touches Africa and Asia — these are the famous "transcontinental" exceptions.

The 5 oceans, largest to smallest

#OceanWhere it is
1PacificThe giant one between Asia and the Americas. Biggest of all.
2AtlanticBetween the Americas and Europe/Africa. The one you cross flying to the US.
3IndianSouth of Asia, between Africa and Australia.
4SouthernThe ring of water around Antarctica.
5ArcticThe frozen cap at the top, around the North Pole.

Three orientation words

Equator — the line round the middle Northern hemisphere — above it (most land & people) Southern hemisphere — below it
